A popular event to watch during the festival is the dragon dance/parade. Groups of trained and/or untrained dancers perform with a dragon on sticks to ward off demons of the past year.



As the day yields to night, festival goers gather to release store-bought or handmade lanterns to the sky. In some countries sky lanterns are banned due to fire hazard; some people will have to settle for hanging lanterns instead.



Dengmi, or lantern riddles, are a popular game during the festival. Classic riddles and puns, often derived from local legends and folklore, are scribbled on sheets of colored papers dangling from storefront lanterns. Children who solve them can get a small prize from the lantern's owner.



Tangyuan are a popular treat traditionally eaten during New Year's festivities, between Lunar New Year and the Lantern Festival. They represent unity and good luck in their sweetness.
